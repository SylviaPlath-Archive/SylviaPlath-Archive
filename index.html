Meet the author

Sylvia Plath (1932-1963) was an accomplished American poet, novelist and short story writer, known for her confessional style and exploration of themes related to personal turmoil, depression and death. Born in Boston, Massachusetts, she was an outstanding student and earned several writing awards during her time at Smith College. After graduation, Plath moved to Cambridge, England, on a Fulbright scholarship and studied at the University of Cambridge. It was there that she met and married fellow poet Ted Hughes, with whom she had two children.
